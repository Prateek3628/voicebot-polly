## 4. Mobile App Development FAQs

### What is the difference between native and cross-platform app development?

Native applications are built for a single platform using languages such as Swift for iOS or Kotlin for Android. Native applications deliver high performance, full access to device features, and a superior user experience.

Cross-platform applications are built using frameworks such as Flutter or React Native, enabling deployment on multiple platforms with one codebase. Cross-platform development reduces development time and cost, with minor performance limitations and restricted access to some native capabilities.

Q: What is the difference between native and cross-platform app development?
A: Native apps are built for one platform (Swift for iOS, Kotlin for Android) with high performance and full device access. Cross-platform apps use frameworks like Flutter or React Native to work on multiple platforms from one codebase, reducing time and cost with minor performance tradeoffs.

---

Cost varies based on complexity. A simple application costs $10,000 to $50,000. A medium complexity application costs $50,000 to $150,000. A complex application costs $200,000 or more.

Q: How much does it cost to develop a mobile app?

### What is an MVP and is it necessary?

An MVP (Minimum Viable Product) includes only the essential features required to validate a concept. Advantages include lower initial investment, faster market entry, real-world user feedback, reduced risk, and iterative improvement based on usage data.

### Is a backend required for a mobile app?

A backend is required for applications involving user authentication, cloud storage, real-time updates, payment processing, analytics, or structured and updated content. Simple offline utilities do not require a backend. Serverless alternatives include Firebase and AWS Amplify.

Q: Does my app need a backend?
A: If your app involves user authentication, cloud storage, real-time updates, payments, or analytics, then yes. Simple offline utility apps do not need a backend. Serverless alternatives like Firebase and AWS Amplify are also options.

### How is a mobile app published?

For the iOS App Store, an Apple Developer account is required at $99 per year. The process involves preparing metadata and screenshots, uploading via App Store Connect, and app review which typically takes 1 to 3 days.

For the Google Play Store, a Google Play Developer account is required at $25 one-time. The process involves uploading the app bundle, preparing the listing, and publication where review is often completed within hours.

Q: How do I publish my app to the App Store and Google Play?
A: For iOS, you need an Apple Developer account ($99/year), prepare metadata, upload via App Store Connect, and wait 1–3 days for review. For Google Play, you need a developer account ($25 one-time), upload the app bundle, and review is usually completed within hours.
